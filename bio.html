Bio-sketch of Kumar Neeraj Verma

I started my training in computer science with strong enthusiasm for research, but soon I felt that the fleeting pleasures offered by developments in modern science and technology do not offer deep and lasting happiness to human beings. To be truly happy, we must ask and find answers to fundamental questions like what is a human being, why must we be born and grow old and die. I became a monk so that I could devote myself fully towards resolving these questions and helping all beings through that.













1979 born in India
1996-2000 BTech, department of computer science and engineering, IIT Delhi
2000-2003 PhD in computer science, ENS Paris-Saclay
2003-2009 computer scientist at Institut fuer Informatik, Technische Universitaet Muenchen, Germany
2009 became postulant Buddhist monk at Mu Sang Sa monastery, South Korea
2011 became novice Buddhist monk in the Jogye Order of Korean Buddhism
2013 became fully ordained Buddhist monk in India in the Myanmar Buddhist tradition. Received name Buddhapragya (बुद्धप्रज्ञा)
