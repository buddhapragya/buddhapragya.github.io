Bio-sketch of Kumar Neeraj Verma

I started my training in computer science with strong enthusiasm for research, but soon I felt that the comforts provided by modern science and technology do not offer deep and lasting happiness to human beings. To be truly happy, we must engage in a deep quest about life, death and the cause and cure of of sorrows. I became a monk so that I could devote myself fully towards resolving these questions and helping all beings through that.

Chronological bio-sketch

1979:           born in India

1996-2000: BTech,
                    department of computer science and engineering, IIT Delhi

2000-2003: PhD in computer science,
                    ENS Paris-Saclay

2003-2009: computer scientist
                    at Institut fuer Informatik,
                    Technische Universitaet Muenchen, Germany

2009:          became postulant Buddhist monk
                    at Mu Sang Sa monastery, South Korea

2011:           became novice Buddhist monk
                    in the Jogye Order of Korean Buddhism

2013:           became fully ordained Buddhist monk in India
                    in the Myanmar Buddhist tradition.
                    Received name Buddhapragya (बुद्धप्रज्ञा)
